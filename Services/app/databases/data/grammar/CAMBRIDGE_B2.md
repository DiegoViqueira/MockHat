# Grammar Reference – B2 Level

## Verb Tenses

- Present simple / continuous / perfect / perfect continuous
- Past simple / continuous / perfect / perfect continuous
- Future forms: will, going to, present continuous, future continuous, future perfect
- Mixed tenses in time clauses
- Future in the past

## Modals and Semi-modals

- Ability: can / could / be able to
- Obligation and necessity: must / have to / need to / should / ought to
- Advice and suggestions: should / ought to / had better / could
- Deduction and speculation: must / might / could / may / can’t
- Criticism and regret: should have / ought to have / might have
- Habits and typical behaviour: will / would / used to

## Conditionals

- Zero, first, second, third conditionals
- Mixed conditionals
- Inversion in conditionals (e.g. Had I known...)
- Alternatives to "if" (unless, provided that, in case, as long as)

## Passive Voice

- All tenses in the passive
- Passive with reporting verbs (It is said that... / He is thought to...)
- Have/get something done
- Passive infinitives and gerunds

## Reported Speech

- Statements, questions, requests, commands
- Changes in verb tenses, time expressions, pronouns
- Reporting verbs: advise, remind, warn, suggest, recommend, etc.
- Reporting with passive structures

## Questions

- Question tags
- Negative questions
- Indirect questions
- Subject vs object questions
- Embedded questions

## Nouns, Articles, and Quantifiers

- Countable vs uncountable nouns
- Articles: a, an, the, zero article
- Specific and general reference
- Much, many, little, few, a lot of, lots of, plenty of

## Pronouns and Determiners

- All types of pronouns (subject, object, reflexive, possessive, relative, demonstrative, indefinite)
- Each / every / all / both / either / neither
- Other / another / the other

## Adjectives and Adverbs

- Gradable and ungradable adjectives
- Adjective + preposition combinations
- Adverbs of frequency, degree, certainty
- Intensifiers (too, very, really, absolutely, etc.)
- Inversion with adverbials (e.g. Never have I seen...)

## Gerunds and Infinitives

- Verb patterns: verb + -ing / to-infinitive / bare infinitive
- Verbs followed by object + infinitive
- Expressions with gerund/infinitive (e.g. It’s no use..., I’d rather..., etc.)
- Gerunds and infinitives after adjectives and nouns

## Relative Clauses

- Defining and non-defining relative clauses
- Reduced relative clauses
- Relative adverbs (where, when, why)
- Omission of relative pronoun

## Linking Devices and Discourse Markers

- Cause and effect: because, since, so, therefore, as a result
- Contrast: although, even though, however, nevertheless, despite
- Addition: moreover, in addition, furthermore, as well as
- Purpose: so that, in order to, to
- Sequencing: firstly, secondly, eventually, finally
- Summarising: in conclusion, to sum up

## Emphasis and Inversion

- Fronting and cleft sentences (e.g. What I like is..., It was her who...)
- Inversion with negative adverbials (e.g. Rarely had I...)
- Emphatic "do" and "did"

## Structures for Speculation and Hypotheses

- Suppose / imagine / what if
- If + past simple / past perfect for hypothetical situations
- I wish / If only (regrets, unreal situations)

## Fixed Phrases and Expressions

- Idiomatic expressions (B2 level)
- Verb + noun collocations
- Common phrasal verbs
- Set phrases in formal/informal contexts

## Style and Register

- Formal vs informal structures
- Use of passive for objectivity
- Nominalisation
- Avoiding repetition with substitution and ellipsis
