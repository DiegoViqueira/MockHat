# Grammar Reference – B1 Level

## Verb Tenses

- Present simple
- Present continuous
- Past simple
- Past continuous
- Present perfect
- Present perfect continuous
- Past perfect
- Will / shall (future)
- Going to (future)
- Future continuous
- Future perfect

## Modal Verbs

- Can / could / be able to
- Must / have to / should / ought to
- Might / may / will / shall
- Would (for advice, offers, preferences)
- Need / needn't / don’t need to
- Had better
- Used to / be used to / get used to

## Conditionals

- Zero conditional
- First conditional
- Second conditional
- Third conditional
- Mixed conditionals
- Wishes and regrets (I wish / If only)
- Should have / could have / would have

## Passive Voice

- Present simple passive
- Past simple passive
- Present perfect passive
- Future passive
- Modal verbs in the passive
- Passive with reporting verbs

## Reported Speech

- Statements
- Questions
- Commands and requests
- Time and place changes in reported speech

## Questions

- Yes / no questions
- Wh- questions
- Question tags
- Indirect questions

## Nouns and Articles

- Countable and uncountable nouns
- A / an / the / zero article
- Quantifiers (some, any, a lot of, much, many, few, little)

## Pronouns and Determiners

- Subject and object pronouns
- Possessive pronouns and adjectives
- Reflexive pronouns
- Demonstratives (this, that, these, those)
- Indefinite pronouns (someone, anybody, nobody, etc.)

## Adjectives and Adverbs

- Comparative and superlative adjectives
- Adjective order
- Adverbs of frequency, manner, degree, time
- Too / enough
- So / such

## Gerunds and Infinitives

- Verb + -ing
- Verb + infinitive (with/without 'to')
- Verb + object + infinitive
- Expressions followed by -ing or to + infinitive

## Relative Clauses

- Defining relative clauses
- Non-defining relative clauses
- Relative pronouns: who, which, that, whose, where, when

## Connectors and Linkers

- Because, so, although, however, therefore
- In spite of / despite
- As soon as, while, until, after, before

## Comparisons

- (not) as ... as
- The ... the ... (e.g. The more, the better)
- Like / as

## Structures for Emphasis

- Cleft sentences (It was ... who / what ...)
- Emphatic structures with "do / does / did"
