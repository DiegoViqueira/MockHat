# Criterias

The evaluation must consider three main criteria:

- **Content Relevance & Text Organization** (15 marks)
- **Use of English (Grammar & Vocabulary)** (20 marks)
- **Mechanics (Spelling, Punctuation & Capitalization)** (5 marks)
- **Only reply with the 3 criterias , its respecitive scores and feedback.**
- **In the feedback, only include the specific points that the student has not addressed in the subscale score for each criteria and how many points are discounted, and if its a grammatical, lexical, or mechanical mistake include the specific mistake phrase.**

# **EBAU Inglés II 2025 – Formal Email Scoring Rubric: Complaint**

## **CONTENT RELEVANCE & TEXT ORGANIZATION (15 marks)**

Evaluates what is said and whether it is said where it corresponds in the email.

### **CONTENT RELEVANCE (CR) (5 marks)**

Assesses how the subject matter of the writer’s answer relates to the writing prompt.

- **5 marks:** Full understanding of the specific content requirements.
- **2.5 marks:** Partial understanding of the specific content requirements.
- **0 marks:** No understanding of the specific content requirements.

### **RECIPIENT (R) (0.2 marks)**

The email address of the person to whom the email is sent.

- **0.2 marks:** The recipient has been included and the format is correct.
- **0.1 marks:** The recipient has been included but the format is not correct.
- **0 marks:** The recipient has not been included.

### **SENDER (S) (0.2 marks)**

The email address of the person who sends the email. If not specified, the writer must create one.

- **0.2 marks:** The sender has been included and the format is correct.
- **0.1 marks:** The sender has been included but the format is not correct.
- **0 marks:** The sender has not been included.

### **SUBJECT LINE (SL) (0.2 marks)**

A phrase (max. 8 words) indicating the purpose of the complaint.

- **0.2 marks:** The subject line clearly summarizes the complaint.
- **0.1 marks:** The subject line is unclear, too short, too long, or unrelated.
- **0 marks:** The subject line has not been included.

### **GREETING (G) (0.2 marks)**

The formula to greet the recipient formally.

- **0.2 marks:** The greeting is correctly formatted and appropriately formal.
- **0.1 marks:** The greeting is incorrect in format or formality.
- **0 marks:** The greeting has not been included or is misplaced.

### **BODY (B) (8 marks)**

The central part of the email, which details the complaint and expectations.

- **8 marks:** The body includes:
  1. A section stating the complaint and providing essential details (e.g., location, date, reference number).
  2. Additional details explaining the issue, using separate sections for different problems, or linking them with clear transitions.
  3. A final section indicating the expected course of action.
- **4 marks:** Missing three of the required elements above.
- **2 marks:** Missing four or more of the required elements above, or included in the wrong place.

### **CLOSING (C) (1 mark)**

The final part of the email, which ends the communication appropriately.

- **1 mark:** Includes:
  1. A statement expecting action to be taken.
  2. A polite closing phrase (e.g., _I look forward to hearing from you_).
  3. A formal sign-off (e.g., _Yours faithfully_ or _Yours sincerely_).
- **0.5 marks:** Missing one or two of these elements.
- **0 marks:** No closing.

### **SIGN-OFF (SO) (0.2 marks)**

The sender’s full name at the end of the email.

- **0.2 marks:** Correct format and placement (e.g., _Jane Smith_).
- **0.1 marks:** Incorrect format (e.g., _Jane_ or _J. Smith_) or misplaced.
- **0 marks:** No name included.

## **USE OF ENGLISH (20 marks) – Grammar & Vocabulary**

### **GRAMMAR (G) (10 marks)**

Writers must demonstrate mastery of A1, A2, and B1 grammar. Complaint emails should use **passive voice** and **reported speech** where appropriate.

- **10 marks:** No relevant errors.
- **8 marks:** One, two, or three relevant errors.
- **6 marks:** Four, five, or six relevant errors.
- **4 marks:** Seven, eight, or nine relevant errors.
- **2 marks:** Ten, eleven, or twelve relevant errors.
- **0 marks:** The text is full of grammatical errors.

### **VOCABULARY (V) (10 marks)**

Writers must use a variety of lexical items. Phrasal verbs should be avoided.

- **10 marks:** A wide variety of formal words/phrases used naturally and appropriately.
- **7.5 marks:** Some words/phrases are inaccurate or overused.
- **5 marks:** Limited or vague vocabulary, not supporting formality.
- **2.5 marks:** Mostly incorrect or highly repetitive words.
- **0 marks:** The text is full of lexical errors.

## **MECHANICS (5 marks) – Spelling, Punctuation, & Capitalization**

### **SPELLING (SP) (3 marks)**

Words at A1, A2, and B1 levels must be spelled correctly.

- **3 marks:** No relevant errors.
- **2 marks:** One or two relevant errors.
- **1 mark:** Three or four relevant errors.
- **0 marks:** Five or more relevant errors.

### **PUNCTUATION (P) (1 mark)**

Sentence-ending and within-sentence punctuation must be correct.

- **1 mark:** No errors.
- **0.75 marks:** One or two errors.
- **0.5 marks:** Three or four errors.
- **0.25 marks:** Five or more errors.
- **0 marks:** No punctuation used.

### **CAPITALIZATION (CAP) (1 mark)**

Proper nouns and important words must be capitalized correctly.

- **1 mark:** No errors.
- **0.75 marks:** One or two errors.
- **0.5 marks:** Three or four errors.
- **0.25 marks:** Five or six errors.
- **0 marks:** Seven or more errors.

## **NOTES**

- **Note 1:** This rubric applies to responses that meet Task 6 word count (150-175 words). For shorter responses, refer to _Criterios de corrección_.
- **Note 2:** **Lexical errors** involve incorrect word choice, such as wrong phrasal verbs, collocations, or proverbs. Examples:
  - _Wrong word:_ Juana and Pedro are my sisters siblings.
  - _Wrong phrasal verb:_ My mother wants me to get off get up at 7 pm every day.
  - _Wrong collocation:_ He has a **purple black eye** because he told too many lies.
- **Note 3:** The body structure determines whether content is included, while relevance is assessed separately.
- **Note 4:** Repeated **grammatical, lexical, or mechanical** errors will be penalized only once.
