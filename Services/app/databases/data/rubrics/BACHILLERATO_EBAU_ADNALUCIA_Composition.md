# EBAU Writing Assessment RUBRIC - Andalucia

## WRITING (3 points)

**WRITE A COMPOSITION OF AT LEAST 120 WORDS ABOUT ONE OF THE TOPICS PROPOSED AND FOCUS ON IT. CHOOSE ONE TOPIC ONLY.**

---

## Evaluation Criteria

The Writing section will be evaluated based on three criteria, each worth one point:

1. **Grammatical accuracy**
2. **Lexical richness and precision**
3. **Textual and communicative aspects**

The score will be assigned in 0.25-point increments depending on how well the composition meets these criteria.

> **Important:** The writing must be based on ONE of the TWO proposed topics ONLY.  
> If the composition does not follow one of the topics, the score will be **0 points**.  
> The text must contain a **minimum of 120 words**. Fewer words will result in a proportionally reduced score.  
> There is no upper word limit, but longer texts will be judged with the same criteria regarding cohesion, precision, and correctness.

---

## Sample Exam Prompts

**BLOCK C (Writing)**  
**Maximum score: 3 points**

Write a composition of at least 120 words about ONE of the two proposed topics ONLY.

C1. Social relations and a nice environment are key to a good lifestyle. How can your neighbourhood improve to become a better place to live in?

C2. More and more people work remotely. What’s your view on that?

## Specific Evaluation Criteria

In this section, the ability to express ideas and communicate in written English will be assessed. The following criteria will be used:

- Grammatical accuracy (repeated errors will be considered only once).
- Lexical richness and precision.
- Textual and communicative aspects.

Each aspect will be graded with 1 / 0.75 / 0.50 / 0.25 / 0 points, depending on the degree of adequacy.

---

### GRAMMATICAL ACCURACY

- Correct word order (e.g., SVOA in affirmative sentence structures).
- No subject-verb or determiner-noun agreement errors.
- Correct use of pronouns.
- Correct use of quantifiers (much, many, etc.).
- Correct use of prepositions.
- Correct formulation of negative structures.
- Correct use of verb tenses.
- No errors in tense formation.
- Correct use of temporal markers (ago, for, since, already, etc.).
- Appropriate use of modals and their correct forms (e.g., use/absence of "to").
- Correct use of definite/indefinite articles in generic/specific contexts.
- Correct use of possessive structures (e.g., Saxon genitive).
- No pluralization of adjectives.
- Knowledge of irregular plurals.

---

### LEXICAL RICHNESS AND PRECISION

- Rich and varied vocabulary; synonyms instead of repetitions.
- Specific and precise word choice.
- No use of Spanish or any other non-English terms.
- No confusion of basic terms (e.g., go/come, learn/teach, lend/borrow, let/leave).
- No confusion of grammatical categories (e.g., live - verb vs. life - noun).
- Avoidance of fillers or overused idioms.

---

### TEXTUAL AND COMMUNICATIVE ASPECTS

- Clear organization and logical sequencing of ideas.
- Text is not repetitive or confusing.
- Clear paragraphing.
- Each paragraph presents a new, relevant, and informative idea without digression.
- Appropriate use of connectors and referential devices.
- Original or coherent approach to the topic.
- Clean and organized presentation with clear handwriting.
- Correct spelling.
- Correct use of punctuation.
- The text demonstrates the student’s maturity and ability to communicate.
- The text shows command of the English language for conveying ideas and presenting arguments.
