# Criterias

The evaluation must consider three main criteria:

- **Content Relevance & Text Organization** (15 marks)
- **Use of English (Grammar & Vocabulary)** (20 marks)
- **Mechanics (Spelling, Punctuation & Capitalization)** (5 marks)
- **Only reply with the 3 criterias , its respecitive scores and feedback.**
- **In the feedback, only include the specific points that the student has not addressed in the subscale score for each criteria and how many points are discounted, and if its a grammatical, lexical, or mechanical mistake include the specific mistake phrase.**

# **EBAU Inglés II 2025 – Informal Email Scoring Rubric**

## **CONTENT RELEVANCE & TEXT ORGANIZATION (15 marks)**

Evaluates what is said and whether it is said where it corresponds in the email.

### **CONTENT RELEVANCE (CR) (5 marks)**

Assesses how the subject matter of the candidate’s answer relates to the writing prompt.

- **5 marks:** Full understanding of the specific content requirements.
- **2.5 marks:** Partial understanding of the specific content requirements.
- **0 marks:** No understanding of the specific content requirements.

### **RECIPIENT (R) (0.2 marks)**

Evaluates if the recipient has been correctly indicated.

- **0.2 marks:** The recipient is indicated and the format is correct.
- **0.1 marks:** The recipient is indicated, but the format is not correct.
- **0 marks:** The recipient has not been indicated.

### **SENDER (S) (0.2 marks)**

Evaluates if the sender has been correctly indicated. If not specified in the instructions, candidates must create one.

- **0.2 marks:** The sender is indicated and the format is correct.
- **0.1 marks:** The sender is indicated, but the format is not correct.
- **0 marks:** The sender has not been indicated.

### **SUBJECT LINE (SL) (0.2 marks)**

A phrase (max. 7 words) briefly indicating what the email is about.

- **0.2 marks:** The subject line clearly summarizes the content of the email.
- **0.1 marks:** The subject line is unclear, too short, too long, or unrelated.
- **0 marks:** The subject line has not been included.

### **GREETING (G) (0.2 marks)**

The formula to greet the recipient.

- **0.2 marks:** The formality level is appropriate for the relationship.
- **0.1 marks:** The formality level is incorrect for the relationship.
- **0 marks:** The greeting has not been included or misplaced.

### **BODY (B) (8 marks)**

The central part of the email, which establishes the purpose and details of the communication.

- **8 marks:** Includes reference to the recipient’s previous email, if applicable, and details the communication goals as per the instructions.
- **4 marks:** Fails to reference the recipient’s email, indicate reasons for the reply, or properly establish communication details.
- **2 marks:** Missing two or more key elements.

### **CLOSING (CL) (1 mark)**

The final part of the email, ending communication appropriately.

- **1 mark:** Includes a closing phrase and a farewell (e.g., _“Take care”_, _“Best wishes”_).
- **0.5 marks:** Missing one of the elements.
- **0 marks:** No closing phrase included.

### **SIGN-OFF (SO) (0.2 marks)**

The sender’s name at the end of the email.

- **0.2 marks:** The sender’s name is correctly formatted (e.g., _“Jane”_).
- **0.1 marks:** The sender’s full name or initials are used (e.g., _“Jane Smith”_, _“J.S.”_).
- **0 marks:** The sender’s name is missing.

## **USE OF ENGLISH (20 marks) – Grammar & Vocabulary**

### **GRAMMAR (G) (10 marks)**

Writers must demonstrate mastery of A1, A2, and B1 grammar.

- **10 marks:** No relevant errors.
- **8 marks:** One, two, or three relevant errors.
- **6 marks:** Four, five, or six relevant errors.
- **4 marks:** Seven, eight, or nine relevant errors.
- **2 marks:** Ten, eleven, or twelve relevant errors.
- **0 marks:** The text is full of grammatical errors.

### **VOCABULARY (V) (10 marks)**

Writers must use a variety of lexical items. Informal variations of formal words (including phrasal verbs) are acceptable. Offensive words will automatically receive the lowest score.

- **10 marks:** A wide variety of topic-related words/phrases used naturally and appropriately.
- **7.5 marks:** A variety of words/phrases used, but some inaccuracies occur.
- **5 marks:** Insufficient development of topic-related words/phrases.
- **2.5 marks:** No topic-related words/phrases or mostly incorrect usage.
- **0 marks:** The text is full of lexical errors.

## **MECHANICS (5 marks) – Spelling, Punctuation, & Capitalization**

### **SPELLING (SP) (3 marks)**

Words at A1, A2, and B1 levels must be spelled correctly.

- **3 marks:** No relevant errors.
- **2 marks:** One or two relevant errors.
- **1 mark:** Three or four relevant errors.
- **0 marks:** Five or more relevant errors.

### **PUNCTUATION (P) (1 mark)**

- **1 mark:** No errors.
- **0.75 marks:** One or two errors.
- **0.5 marks:** Three or four errors.
- **0.25 marks:** Five or more errors.
- **0 marks:** No punctuation used.

### **CAPITALIZATION (CAP) (1 mark)**

Words with capital letters (e.g., names, brands, countries, holidays) must be correctly capitalized.

- **1 mark:** No errors.
- **0.75 marks:** One or two errors.
- **0.5 marks:** Three or four errors.
- **0.25 marks:** Five or six errors.
- **0 marks:** Seven or more errors.

## **NOTES**

- **Note 1:** This rubric is for answers meeting Task 6 requirements (150-175 words). For answers between 150-120 words or fewer than 120 words, follow correction instructions in _Criterios de corrección_.
- **Note 2:** A **lexical error** involves incorrect word selection (e.g., wrong phrasal verb, collocation, or proverb). Examples:
  - _Wrong word:_ Juana and Pedro are my sisters siblings.
  - _Wrong phrasal verb:_ My mother wants me to get off get up at 7 pm every day.
  - _Wrong collocation:_ He has a **purple black eye** because he told too many lies.
- **Note 3:** Including details in the body is different from ensuring content relevance. The _body_ section assesses whether content is present, while _content relevance_ evaluates how well it aligns with the prompt.
- **Note 4:** Repeated instances of the same **grammatical, lexical, or mechanical** error will only be penalized once.
